<section class="slide" id="use-the-pi-zero-when-you"><div><h2>Use the Pi Zero when you:</h2>

<ul>
  <li>don’t need <strong>analog input</strong></li>
  <li>want to use existing <strong>linux libraries</strong></li>
  <li>have a device that will stay <strong>on all the time</strong></li>
  <li>need <strong>fast</strong> digital electronics communication (and can program in C)</li>
  <li>need a lot of <strong>storage</strong> or <strong>RAM</strong></li>
</ul>

</div></section>
<section class="slide" id="use-the-teensy-when-you"><div><h2>Use the Teensy when you:</h2>

<ul>
  <li>need <strong>analog I/O</strong> or a lot of <strong>digital I/O</strong></li>
  <li>need to <strong>security audit</strong> your code</li>
  <li>will be turning your <strong>device on and off</strong> frequently</li>
</ul>

</div></section>
<section class="slide" id="micropython-an-alternative-to-c"><div><h2>MicroPython: an alternative to C++</h2>

<ul>
  <li>original kickstarter by Damien George, a <strong>physicist</strong> at Cambridge</li>
  <li>used by the BBC micro:bit</li>
  <li>compatible with the Teensy 3.+ (but not the LC… yet?)</li>
  <li>stripped-down standard library</li>
</ul>

</div></section>
